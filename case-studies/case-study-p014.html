Voice-to-Email: Executive Productivity Automation

A 9-node n8n workflow that takes a voice recording as input and delivers a clean, formatted, ready-to-review email draft as output. Built for executives who think faster than they type.

The Problem

Senior executives, coaches, and business owners typically spend 2-4 hours a day on email. A significant portion of that time is not spent thinking about what to say. It is spent typing, formatting, re-reading, and editing. The thinking is fast. The output is slow.

Voice notes solve half the problem. People can dictate much faster than they type. But a voice note is not an email. It needs to be transcribed, structured, given a subject line, formatted for professional delivery, and composed into the email client.

The gap between "I know what I want to say" and "the email is in the recipient's inbox" is where executive time goes.

An executive who can speak a 90-second note and have a polished email drafted in 30 seconds gains back hours every week. That is the value of bridging voice to action.

How It Works

Nine nodes. Each one has a single responsibility. The pipeline is sequential, with a human review gate at the end before anything sends.

Node 1: TriggerThe workflow accepts a voice file input via webhook, file upload, or connected mobile app.
Node 2: Whisper TranscriptionThe audio file is sent to OpenAI Whisper for transcription. Handles accents, filler words, and varying audio quality.
Node 3: CleanupA code node removes filler words, false starts, and repeated phrases. Produces clean text input for the AI writer.
Node 4: Context ExtractionGPT-4o reads the cleaned transcription and extracts: intended recipient, email purpose, action items, deadlines, and overall tone.
Node 5: Draft GenerationUsing the extracted context, GPT-4o writes a complete email: subject line, greeting, body, closing, and signature placeholder. Tone matches the spoken input.
Node 6: Quality CheckA second AI pass reviews the draft for clarity, completeness, and professional tone. If anything critical was omitted, it flags and regenerates.
Node 7: FormatThe approved draft is formatted into clean HTML for email delivery.
Node 8: Gmail DraftThe formatted email is saved as a Gmail draft. Not sent. The executive reviews, edits if needed, and sends.
Node 9: NotificationA confirmation message is sent to the executive: "Your draft is ready. Subject: [subject line]. Open Gmail to review and send."

Why Drafts, Not Auto-Send

The system saves to drafts rather than sending automatically. This is not a technical limitation — it is a deliberate design decision.

Email sent by an AI under an executive's name is a reputation risk. The value is not removing the human from the loop. It is removing the human from the slow, mechanical part: transcription, formatting, and composition. The final review and send stays human.

The executive goes from spending 15 minutes composing an email to spending 60 seconds reviewing a draft and pressing send.
