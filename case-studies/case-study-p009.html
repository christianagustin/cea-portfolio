The Problem

Your best salespeople are spending hours every week doing work a computer could do in minutes. They open Google Maps, search for businesses in a category, manually visit each website, hunt for an email address, check if the business is registered and active, and type it all into a spreadsheet.

After all that, roughly half the leads turn out to be existing customers they did not know about. The other half are missing critical information so the rep has to go back and do more research before making a call.

The result: top salespeople spend 30-40% of their time prospecting instead of selling. That is not a hustle problem. It is a systems problem.

The best salespeople close deals. They should not be building spreadsheets.

How It Works

Five separate workflows. Each one has one job. They can be debugged, updated, or re-run independently.

Workflow 1: DiscoveryA form accepts an industry keyword and lead count. The workflow queries Google Maps for matching businesses in Singapore. Results are cleaned, standardized, and deduplicated against existing rows using Place ID as the key. Only new businesses are appended.
Workflow 2: Registration LookupFor each new lead, the system looks up whether the business is formally registered and active. It searches the national business registry by name, scores multiple results by name similarity and registration status, and writes the registration number back to the row.
Workflow 3: Website Contact ExtractionFor each lead with a website, the system visits the homepage, contact page, and about page. It extracts emails and validates mobile numbers by format — correct prefix and length only. Results go into a separate column from the Maps phone number.
Workflow 4: Platform Profile DetectionAn optional step checks whether each business has a profile on the target marketplace platform via a scoped Google search. If a profile exists, the sales conversation changes — it is a reactivation call, not cold outreach.
Workflow 5: Existing Client Cross-ReferenceOn a scheduled hourly run, new leads are compared against the master client list matching on registration number and phone. Any match is flagged as Skip - Existing Client and excluded from the active pipeline.

Design Decisions

No AI in core pipelineAI adds per-lead token cost without improving accuracy for structured lookups. Direct scraping with regex scoring keeps enrichment cost at fractions of a cent per lead.
Place ID as dedup keyBusiness names change, addresses move, phone numbers recycle. The Maps Place ID is a stable, unique identifier that survives rebrands.
Two phone columnsThe Maps phone is often a landline or reception. The website phone is more often a direct mobile. Keeping them separate gives the rep more options.
Hourly client cross-referenceThe client master list updates throughout the day. Running the cross-reference on a schedule rather than per-lead keeps the pipeline clean without slowing discovery.
